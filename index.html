ZOO ANIMALS

1. Bears

Bears are large, powerful mammals known for their thick fur, strong limbs, and distinctive, broad heads. They are primarily omnivorous, with diets that include fruits, nuts, fish, and occasionally small mammals. Bears are found in a variety of habitats, from forests and mountains to tundras and grasslands. They are solitary creatures, except when raising cubs, and are known for their hibernation habits during winter in colder climates.

2. Giraffes

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, recognizable by their long necks, distinctive coat patterns, and long, slender legs. Native to the savannas of Africa, they primarily feed on leaves, fruits, and flowers of tall trees, especially acacias. Giraffes are social animals often found in loosely connected herds. Despite their height, they are agile and can run at speeds of up to 35 mph (56 km/h).

3. Lions

Lions are large, muscular felines known as the "king of the jungle" due to their powerful build and majestic manes (in males). They are social animals living in prides, which consist of related females, their offspring, and a few males. Native to Africa, lions are apex predators that hunt in groups, mainly targeting large ungulates like zebras and antelopes. They are known for their deep roars that can be heard up to 5 miles (8 km) away.

4. Monkeys

Monkeys are highly intelligent and social primates found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world. They vary widely in size, appearance, and behavior, with some living in trees and others on the ground. Monkeys are known for their dexterous hands, expressive faces, and complex social structures. Their diets are diverse, including fruits, leaves, insects, and sometimes small animals. They communicate using vocalizations, facial expressions, and body language.

5. Alligators

Alligators are large reptiles known for their broad, powerful bodies, long tails, and distinctive snouts. They are primarily found in freshwater habitats such as swamps, rivers, and lakes in the southeastern United States and China. Alligators are apex predators that feed on fish, birds, mammals, and other reptiles. They are known for their stealthy hunting style, using their camouflage to ambush prey. Despite their fearsome appearance, they are generally shy around humans.
